Isupati Maa Initiative, is a registered non-governmental organization (NGO), established in Kenya on January 14th,2025, by Ivy Simaton,under registration number CGK/DSS/CBO/40034/22.

INSPIRATION

Witnessing how the Maasai community proudly preserves its rich heritage through traditional dress, language, and ceremonies inspired me to create an initiative dedicated to empowering indigenous communities. Our goal is to help them document and pass down their cultural knowledge to future generations, while also equipping members of the Maa community to become ambassadors in health and agriculture—bridging cultural pride with scientific progress.

MISSION

To empower the Maasai community through integrated initiatives in education, healthcare, and economic development—ensuring access to quality learning, improved health outcomes, and sustainable livelihoods for a brighter, more equitable future.

VISION

A thriving Maasai community empowered by education, equitable healthcare, and sustainable economic opportunities—fostering self-reliance, resilience, and shared prosperity while preserving its rich cultural heritage.

VALUES

At Isupati Maa Initiative, we are guided by the values of lifelong learning, empowerment, and innovation. We are committed to integrity, accountability, and sustainability in all our actions, striving to create a meaningful impact on both communities and the environment. We champion diversity, inclusion, and collaboration—building strong, supportive networks while fostering resilience, ethical leadership, and determination to navigate challenges and drive lasting change.

GOALS

Isupati Maa Initiative seeks to deliver high-quality education and vocational training that equips individuals with practical, industry-relevant skills. Our aim is to foster lifelong learning and career advancement while cultivating leadership, creativity, and collaborative problem-solving. We are dedicated to promoting inclusive growth, particularly for underrepresented groups, and to creating measurable social and economic impact. Through a strong emphasis on sustainability and cultural preservation, we prepare community members to lead with purpose and address real-world challenges with confidence and compassion.

FUNDING STRATEGIES

To sustain and grow its programs, Isupati Maa Initiative employs a diverse and strategic approach to fundraising. These strategies are designed to engage both local and global supporters, ensuring long-term impact and financial stability:

Crowdfunding & Community Fundraisers

1.We leverage online platforms like GoFundMe, GlobalGiving, and M-Changa to run targeted campaigns for specific projects (e.g., building a school, launching a mobile health clinic). Local and diaspora-based fundraising events—such as cultural nights, charity walks, and auctions—can also mobilize grassroots support and raise awareness.

Corporate Sponsorships & Strategic Partnerships

2. We engage businesses, philanthropic organizations, and foundations to invest in the Initiative’s programs. These partnerships can include direct funding, in-kind support, joint campaigns, and CSR alignment for companies looking to create impact in rural Kenya.

Grants & Institutional Funding

3. We will apply for grants from international development agencies (e.g., USAID, UNICEF, WHO, Gates Foundation) and African regional bodies that fund education, healthcare, and community development projects. This includes both program-specific and capacity-building grants.

Equity-Free Investment & Social Impact Funding

4. We will target social impact investors and innovation funds that support community-driven projects. Although Isupati Maa is not for-profit, collaboration with mission-aligned investors in areas like clean water, provision of healthcare services tailored to the needs of the Masai community or sustainable agriculture can unlock growth opportunities.

Support from Well-Wishers & Individual Donors

5. We will cultivate relationships with individual donors, especially from the Kenyan diaspora and global friends of the Maasai community. Personalized campaigns, donor appreciation programs, and regular impact updates can help retain and grow this base.

Merchandise & Cultural Tourism

6. We will create revenue through the sale of branded merchandise, Maasai crafts, or offering cultural immersion experiences in partnership with ethical tourism operators—generating both income and visibility.

Membership & Volunteer Programs

7. We will introduce tiered membership plans for supporters and professionals who want to contribute regularly, and launching structured volunteer programs with optional donation components.
